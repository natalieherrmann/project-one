About

I'm Natalie Herrmann, a fourth-year undergraduate at Illinois Institute of Technology majoring in Psychology and minoring in Computer Science. The intersection between psychology and technology, or cyberpsychology, is my main interest.

There is a lot of potential in technologyâ€”it can be used to increase accessibility, engagement, convenience, and connections. But, it can also be overwhelming and addicting, inducing nomophobia or a fear of missing out. The way we interact with technology and its ability to benefit or hurt us is fascinating to me.

My interest in this area first sparked after my first computer science class, which emphasized that one must always consider the user when creating a program. I truly enjoyed taking a user-centered design approach and all the programming I had to do for assignments. After that class, I began pursuing a minor in Computer Science and became a Teaching Assistant for it for the following two semesters.
